Emergency Mental Health Contacts

In case of immediate danger or crisis, please reach out to the appropriate services below.

National Emergency Services

Police or Ambulance

If someone is in immediate danger or requires urgent medical attention, call emergency services right away.

Call 911
National Suicide Prevention Hotline

Speak with a trained counselor available 24/7 for confidential support.

Call 1-800-273-TALK
Crisis Text Line

Connect with a crisis counselor via text messaging for support during a mental health crisis.

Text HELLO to 741741

Local Support Organizations

NAMI Helpline

National Alliance on Mental Illness offers resources, support groups, and education programs.

Call 1-800-950-NAMI
SAMHSA National Helpline

Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration provides treatment referral and support.

Call 1-800-662-HELP
Trevor Project

Crisis intervention and suicide prevention services for LGBTQ+ youth.

Call 1-866-488-7386

Tips for Supporting Others

Stay Calm and Present

During a crisis, remain calm and reassuring. Show empathy and avoid making judgmental statements.

Encourage Professional Support

Help connect the individual to professional counselors, therapists, or crisis services when possible.

Be Consistent

Check in with them regularly and offer ongoing support, even after the immediate crisis has passed.
