Help Others on Their Mental Wellness Journey

Learn how to support the people in your life with understanding, empathy, and actionable steps.

Recognizing the Signs

Changes in Behavior

Look for noticeable changes such as withdrawal from social activities, loss of interest in hobbies, or increased irritability.

Physical Symptoms

Be aware of complaints such as persistent fatigue, frequent headaches, or noticeable weight changes.

Unusual Statements

Pay attention to expressions of hopelessness, feelings of worthlessness, or talk of being a burden.

How to Offer Support

Listen Without Judgment

Be an active and empathetic listener. Allow them to express their feelings without fear of criticism or dismissal.

  • Maintain eye contact to show you’re present.
  • Avoid interrupting or offering solutions too quickly.
  • Use phrases like, "I’m here for you," or "I hear you."
Encourage Professional Help

Gently suggest reaching out to a therapist, counselor, or doctor for professional guidance. Provide reassurance that seeking help is a sign of strength.

Stay Patient and Supportive

Recovery takes time. Be patient, and continue showing your support, even if progress seems slow.

Practical Actions You Can Take

Check In Regularly

Send a quick message or make a call to ask how they’re doing. Consistent check-ins show that you care.

Offer Help with Tasks

Help them manage everyday tasks like cooking, cleaning, or running errands, especially during tough times.

Learn About Mental Health

Educate yourself about mental health conditions to better understand what your loved one may be experiencing.
